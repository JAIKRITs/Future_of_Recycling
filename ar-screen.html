Metals inside your device
The AR overlay highlights where key metals are located on the PCB. Visitors tap a glowing hotspot to learn what each metal does and why recycling it matters.
Gold (Au) — connectors, high value
Copper (Cu) — tracks & coils
Silver (Ag) — contact points
Palladium (Pd) — capacitors
  • Tap a hotspot to see name, use and recovery potential.
  • Colour-coded overlay: yellow = gold, orange = copper, blue = silver, violet = palladium.
  • Short, bilingual labels (English + Hindi/Gujarati) ensure clarity for all visitors.
Hazard view
In hazard mode, AR dims the metals and makes toxic zones glow red, with simple warnings about health and environmental risks if the device is burned or dumped.
  • Lead (Pb): damages nervous system if released into soil and dust.
  • Mercury (Hg): contaminates water and enters the food chain.
  • Brominated flame retardants: form toxic dioxins when burned.
Audio alert: “Don’t burn e-waste in open air.”
Guidance: “Take it to an authorised recycler.”
Recycling steps (simplified)
A step-by-step timeline shows how a phone moves through dismantling and recovery, linking directly to what visitors see on the AR table.
  1. Check & reuse: if the phone still works, donate or reuse instead of recycling.
  2. Manual dismantling: remove battery, screen, PCB, cables, plastics.
  3. Pre-processing: shredding + magnetic / density separation to concentrate metals.
  4. Metallurgy: hydrometallurgy / bioleaching to extract gold, copper and silver.
  5. Refined metals: feed into new electronics — a circular loop instead of landfill.
